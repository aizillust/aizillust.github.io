Sine Fine

Short Video Response Project

Watch on YouTube

“Sine Fine,” meaning without end in Latin, explores the overconsumption of digital media and the way platforms like TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, and YouTube use chance and play to keep us endlessly scrolling. Each swipe presents the illusion of new possibility, yet the cycle always returns to the same place: distraction, loss of focus, and time slipping away. The video’s upward scrolling motion mirrors the structure of an infinite feed, reinforcing the feeling of a loop that never truly resolves. Throughout the piece, familiar objects become symbolic of how daily life collapses into the cycle of consumption: a Word document erasing itself reflects productivity dissolving; the clothing rack, printer, and scanner evoke mechanical repetition; and an upside-down piggy bank becomes a metaphor for wasted time and energy. The longest section which is the browsing of Netflix, highlights the paralysis of indecision, where even choosing what to watch becomes another form of entrapment. A brief moment of silence toward the end offers a fleeting pause of awareness, a chance to step outside the loop. Yet the return of the reels and the closing card looping back to the beginning complete the cycle, making the viewer not just witness repetition but feel it. Video Length: 03:02 Filmed on Phone + Tripod Edited on Premiere Pro & After Effects
