The Offering

Video Performance-Theme:Actions

Watch on YouTube

The Offering continues my exploration of digital overconsumption by shifting focus from the screen to the body that serves it. Where Sine Fine examined the loop of endless scrolling, this piece concentrates on the labor and energy we surrender to our devices. Through a ritual of feeding, the work makes visible what media consumption quietly demands. The central metaphor is nourishment. Instead of feeding myself, I offer rice, water, coins, and torn notebook pages to an iPad, treating the device as if it were alive and dependent on my care. Performed slowly and deliberately, these actions blur the line between devotion and exhaustion. My body becomes secondary: I perform the work, but the screen receives everything. The yellow tint enhances the tension of this ritual, turning everyday tasks into something intimate and oppressive. Each element carries symbolic weight. Coins taken from the piggy bank echo Sine Fine, transforming saved value into sacrificed value. Torn notebook pages symbolize creativity consumed by the screen. Occasional POV shots from the iPad reverse the viewer’s perspective, suggesting that the device watches and shapes us. Ambient sounds like coins clinking, paper tearing,and water pouring creates a structure of ritual and heighten its unease. The final overhead shot reveals imbalance: the area in front of the device is cluttered and overflowing, while my own plate and the others remain perfectly clean. Nothing is left for myself. The Offering reflects how digital overconsumption does not merely distract us but drains us, leaving our time, attention, and creativity spent at the feet of the screen.
