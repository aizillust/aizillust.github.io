Resonance

Video & Sound Art

Watch on YouTube

Resonance builds on the ideas from Signal Noise by pairing its soundscape with visuals that reflect how overstimulation affects memory, focus, and presence. The video starts with a simple walk across campus, placing the viewer in a familiar setting. Once the ringtone cuts the scene to black, the piece shifts into a more internal, emotional space. The video uses breathing as its main visual rhythm. Each inhale and exhale moves the viewer between different locations: looking up at the sky, inside the Legion of Honor, or standing at the art building. These breathing transitions show moments of clarity and reflection, contrasted with moments where attention drifts. When the heartbeat enters the sound, the visuals change into a sequence of still images from my walk to school. Each heartbeat advances the frame, turning an ordinary routine into a fragmented memory. As the heartbeat speeds up, the images shift faster and become mixed with flashes of daily life, digital interactions, and past experiences, representing how everything blends together when overstimulation peaks. The video eventually slows down and returns to the opening scene, creating a loop. This repetition reflects how attention cycles constantly between awareness and distraction.
